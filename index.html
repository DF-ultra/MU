Mekelle University (MU) is a federal government-funded higher education institution located in Mekelle, Tigray Region, Ethiopia, approximately 783 km north of Addis Ababa. It was officially established in May 2000 through the merger of two former institutions: the Mekelle Business College and Mekelle University College.

It is one of the largest public universities in Ethiopia, recognized for its commitment to teaching, research, and community engagement with an international reputation.

üéØ Mission & Vision

Mission: MU is a research-intensive institution committed to pursuing excellence in research and innovation, teaching-learning, and community engagement. Its ultimate goal is to contribute to the advancement of knowledge, economic growth, and social welfare to national and international communities.

Vision: Mekelle University aspires to become one of the top 25 research-intensive universities in Africa by 2025.

üìö Academic Structure

The university is structured across several colleges and institutes, running a diverse range of programs (undergraduate, master's, and PhD).

Main Colleges:

  • College of Business and Economics
  • College of Dryland Agriculture and Natural Resources Management
  • College of Health Sciences (including Ayder Referral Hospital)
  • College of Law and Governance
  • College of Natural and Computational Sciences
  • College of Social Sciences and Languages
  • College of Veterinary Science

Key Institutes include:

  • Ethiopian Institute of Technology - Mekelle (EiT-M)
  • Institute of Paleo-Environment and Heritage Conservation
  • Institute of Geo-Information and Earth Observation
  • Institute of Water and Environment

üí° Key Focus Areas

MU is known for its strong focus on Dryland Agriculture and Natural Resources Management, reflecting the ecological context of its region. The university actively engages in community services, including offering free legal aid and capacity building for local farmers in areas like conservative agriculture and irrigation.
